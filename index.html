Islam is a religion that means "submission to the will of God" in Arabic.
Muslims, the followers of Islam, believe in one God, Allah, and that the final prophet was Muhammad.
Here are some key beliefs and practices of Islam:
The Quran: The Quran is the primary holy text of Islam, and Muslims base their laws on it.
The Sunnah: The Sunnah is a practical example of the Prophet Muhammad's life.
The Five Pillars of Islam: The Five Pillars of Islam are the declaration of faith, praying five times a day, giving money to charity, fasting, and making a pilgrimage to Mecca.
Holy places: Some important Islamic holy places include the Kaaba shrine in Mecca, the Al-Aqsa mosque in Jerusalem, and the Prophet Muhammad's mosque in Medina.
Other prophets: Muslims also respect other prophets, including Jesus, Moses, and Abraham.
Monotheism: Muslims are monotheistic, meaning they believe in one God.
Free will: Muslims believe that humans have free will, but nothing can happen without Allah's permission.
